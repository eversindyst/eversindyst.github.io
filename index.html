Table of contents
What is my quest? The goal of Argtura is to defeat the High King, Zurguran. The game will play itself for the most part, handling movement, combat, and looting. You will have to manage your character to ensure you are able to complete your quest. You have the ability to select which zone your character explores, what gear your character wears, and what skills and spells they have at their arsenal.
What are my stats? Your character has the following Stats:
Level
Your Level is an indicator of how strong you are. When exploring a new zone be sure to use your level as a reference for the zone's difficulty.

Strength
Your Strength is a measurement of how powerful you are. Every 2 Strength increases your Base Damage by 1 and every 5 Strength increases your HP by 1.

Intelligence
Your Intelligence indicates your mental capacity. Every 2 Intelligence increases your Spell Damage by 1% and every 5 Intelligence increases your Mana by 2.

Dexterity
Dexterity is how swift and accurate you are. Every 2 Dexterity increases your Stamina by 1 and every 5 Dexterity increases your Critical Strike Chance by 1 and your Critical Strike Damage by 5.

Endurance
Your Endurance is a measurement of how much your body can endure. Every 5 Endurance increases your Stamina and HP by 1 and every 10 Endurance increases your Armor by 1%.

Wisdom
Wisdom is a measurement of problem solving and reasoning skills. Every 5 Wisdom increases your Mana by 2 and every 10 Wisdom increases your Resist by 1%.

Agility
Your Agility is a measurement of how quick and fleet you are. Every 2 Agility increases your Stamina and Mana by 1 and every 10 Agility increases your Evasion by 1%.

Armor
Armor reduces the physical damage you take. Physical damage is color coded white.

Evasion
Evasion is the chance that you will avoid a basic attack from an enemy.

Resist
Resist reduces all damage you take. It is a multiplicative bonus on top of Armor and Elemental Resists.

Fire Resist
Fire Resist reduces the fire damage you take. Fire damage is color coded red.

Cold Resist
Cold Resist reduces the cold damage you take. Cold damage is color coded cyan.

Shock Resist
Shock Resist reduces the shock damage you take. Shock damage is color coded yellow.

Life Leech
Life Leech converts a percent of your basic attack's damage into HP.

HP
HP is how many Health Points you have. If this reaches 0, you die.

Mana
Mana is a resource primarily used for casting spells.

Stamina
Stamina is a resource primarily used for moving and skills.

Damage
Your Base Damage is the starting point for how much damage your basic attack will do.

Spell Damage
Spell Damage increases the potency of your spells.

Critical Strike Chance
This is the chance that your attacks, skills, and spells will land a critical strike.

Critical Strike Damage
How much more damage your critical strikes deal.

Fire Damage
Fire Damage increases the fire damage you deal. Fire damage is color coded red.

Cold Damage
Cold Damage increases the cold damage you deal. Cold damage is color coded cyan.

Shock Damage
Shock Damage increases the shock damage you deal. Shock damage is color coded yellow.

Dark Damage
Dark Damage increases the dark damage you deal. Dark damage is color coded purple.

Platinum
Platinum is the standard currency used in the world of Argtura.

Experience to Level
This is how many Experience Points you need to reach the next level.
How do I get stronger?
Leveling As you kill enemies and gain XP you will eventually Level Up. Upon leveling up you will be awarded a talent point. This is indicated by a red + button showing up beside the "SKILL" button on the right. Clicking this button will bring up a talent tree menu where you can select from your available talents. These are flat stat increases that will help you throughout your journey. Initially there are 3 lines of upgrades to select from. Each line has 65 total choices. Every 5 choices will be a stronger than normal upgrade. Once all choices have been made in an upgrade line, that line will go away.
Skills and Spells
Equipment Slain enemies have a chance to drop equipment on their death. Each enemy has a different limit on how many items it can drop at once, along with which items and how rare those items are. Equipment can be worn or removed from the "GEAR" menu. Clicking an item in your inventory will equip the item. Clicking an item that's equip will remove the item.
Hovering over an item will show you the slot that the item goes in along with its stats. When you collect a duplicate of an item, the duplicate will be destroyed and grant some XP to the original version of the item. Once the original reaches 100 XP it will Level Up into a stronger version of itself. When an item reaches level 5 it will transform into a new more powerful item.

Your inventory can be sorted in in Alphabetical order, by the slot type, or by the level of the items. You can also click the "SELL" button to enter sell mode. While in sell mode clicking an item in your inventory will sell it. Items are worth 0 Platinum. If you reacquire a sold item it will retain its previous experience and level.
Multiclass Upon reaching Level 40 a new menu will show up called "CLASS". This menu will bring up the multiclass screen. Multiclassing will allow you to change into another class to further improve your power. There is a Platinum cost associated wtih multiclassing that will increase each time you multiclass. Each mutliclass has a base class tied to it. You can only be each base class once except for your starting class. Once you have been each base class you will be able to multiclass one final time back to your original base class.

Example: If you start as the Mage class, the first time you multiclass you will be able to choose from a Warrior, Thief, or Monk base class. If you pick Warrior then the next time you multiclass you will only be able to choose from a Thief or Monk base class.

When you multiclass you will be reset to Level 1 and your item progress will also be reset. You do not keep your bonuses from your talent tree. Each multiclass will have bonuses that are only present while you are still that class along with bonuses that will last through multiclassing.

How does it all work?
Exploring Your character will explore the current zone automatically. The character will explore every room in the zone and then backtrack to the start. While exploring the zone it will encounter monsters and will do battle with them. Once the character has fully explored the zone and made it back to the starting room the zone will be marked complete on the world map.

The world map can be accessed with the "MAP" button on the far right. This will bring up a menu showing the different zone types and which zones you have unlocked for each type. A red zone means it has not been clearned yet while a green zone means it has been cleared. Hovering over a zone will give some information about the zone. New zones become unlocked based on which zones are cleared. Different zone types require different amounts of Stamina to traverse. If the character dies while in a zone, the zone will be reset when they revive. To clear a zone the character has to explore all of it without dying.
Combat
Basic Combat is performed once a round. Rounds occur every 2 seconds. During a round of combat the following happen:
1. The player will attack the first enemy in the room with a basic attack
2. The player will then attempt to cast every skill/spell it has equipped
3. If a enemy is defeated the player will be rewarded gold and experience
4. Remaining enemies will attack the player with their basic attack
5. If the player's HP is reduced to 0 they will die
Status Effects Skills and Spells can apply status effects to enemies. Each Skill and Spell have their own chance to apply a status effect and their own duration for applied status effects.
Stun will cause the enemy to be unable to act for that turn
Weaken will cause the enemy's damage to be cut in half for that turn
Vulnerable will cause the enemy to take 50% more damage for that turn
Advanced When calculating damage all bonuses and increases are applied first. Then for every target, a check is performed to see if the attack will miss (Spells and Skills cannot miss). This is based on the targets evasion rating. If the attack does not miss then the calculated damage is tested against the targets defenses to determine the total damage done.

Each damage type performs differently:

Physical damage has a range of +/- 30% of the base value. It is then reduced by Armor and further reduced by Resist.

Fire damage deals 1% more damage per 1% of the targets max HP the hit would originally do. Example, a hit for 50 damage on a target with 100 max HP will take 75 damage before resistances.

Cold damage penetrates 1 Cold Resist per 1% of the targets max HP the hit would originally do. Example, a hit for 50 damage on a target with 100 max HP will take 75 damage at 0 Cold Resist and 50 damage at 50 Cold Resist.

Shock damage can deal anywhere from 5% to 280% of the initial hit before resistances. Example a 50 base hit can range between 3 and 189 damage before resistances.

Dark damage is not reduced by the Resist stat.
Combat Terms During combat your character and the enemies will display a status of how they are doing. When they attack they will also show how powerful an attack is. The color and words associated with attacks are based off of the targets max HP

tickles: the attack did less than 3% of the targets max HP
sratches: the attack did less than 7%, but more than 2% of the targets max HP
scrapes: the attack did less than 10%, but more than 6% of the targets max HP
injures: the attack did less than 13%, but more than 9% of the targets max HP
wounds: the attack did less than 16%, but more than 12% of the targets max HP
ravages: the attack did less than 19%, but more than 15% of the targets max HP
decimates: the attack did less than 22%, but more than 18% of the targets max HP
massacres: the attack did less than 28%, but more than 21% of the targets max HP
devastates: the attack did less than 36%, but more than 27% of the targets max HP
annihilates: the attack did less than 45%, but more than 35% of the targets max HP
destroys: the attack did more than 44% of the targets max HP

on the verge of death: the target is below 9% max HP
gushing tons of blood: the target is between 8% and 16% max HP
wishing the blood and pain would stop: the target is between 15% and 21% max HP
covered in bloody wounds and bruises: the target is between 20% and 31% max HP
limping around begging for help: the target is between 30% and 50% max HP
visibly in pain: the target is between 49% and 66% max HP
in okay condition: the target is between 65% and 79% max HP
doing fine: the target is between 78% and 91% max HP
in great condition: the target is above 90% max HP
Death/Resting By default, exploring a zone will consume Stamina. If you do not have enough Stamina to travel to the next room your character will enter resting mode. During resting mode they will sit down and wait until their Stamina is back to max. While resting, Stamina Regeneration is increased by 300%. Basic attacks and Skills in combat use up Stamina. If you do not have enough Stamina to perform that action you will do nothing on that turn. There are ways to reduce or change the stat that is used to perform movement, basic attacks, and skills/spells.

If your character's HP hits 0 in combat they will die. While your character is dead all they will be able to do is lie there until they have regenerated all of their HP and then they will be revived. Upon revival they will start the current zone over again.
